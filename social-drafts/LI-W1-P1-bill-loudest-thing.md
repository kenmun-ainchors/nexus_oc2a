# LI-W1-P1 — "The day my AI bill became the loudest thing in the room"

## Slot
Tue 16 Jun 07:30 AEST

## Draft

---

Some time back, the cost of running my AI workflows bent upwards sharply. I didn't notice immediately.

It started with a model change. A small one. A few workflows switched to a different tier because the previous one had quality issues I'd been working around for too long.

The new model was meaningfully more expensive per call. That part I knew.

What I didn't know: it was also pulling far more context per call. And the workflows I'd built were already running hot on token use.

The compound hit landed fast. Same volume of work. Outsized cost. And the quality didn't go up. It went sideways - slower responses, weirder outputs, more retries.

The first sign wasn't a number. It was a standup where I realised I was explaining around the system instead of using it.

Cost is the loudest signal. When the bill changes trajectory, you don't have a quality problem. You have a foundation problem the cost is exposing.

Watch the bill. When the line bends, the foundation is moving underneath you. The number is the symptom.
