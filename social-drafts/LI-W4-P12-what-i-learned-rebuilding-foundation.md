## Draft

Earlier my AI workflow was held together by a strong model. Today it is held together by the foundation underneath.

Here is what I learned.

Strong models hide weak foundations. The longer the model covers for the system, the bigger the debt when the model changes. I have paid that debt. I do not recommend it.

Cost is a signal, not just a number. When the trajectory bends, the foundation is moving. Watch the line, not the point. The bill tells you what the system cannot.

Audit before you fix. You cannot rebuild a foundation you have not measured. I thought I knew my system. The audit proved I did not. That was the most valuable hour I spent.

Discipline beats motivation, every time. Build the process. Do not rely on the person. The person changes. The system does not.

Specs are the new prompts. The work is the spec, not the model. The model is replaceable. The spec is yours.

If you are building with AI right now and the system feels almost working, the answer is not more prompts. It is a foundation. The work is upstream of the tool.

That is what the silence was for.

If you have rebuilt a foundation underneath a system that was almost working, I would love to hear what you kept and what you tore out. Reply with one thing. I will share mine in the thread.
