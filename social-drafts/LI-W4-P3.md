# Why Your AI Rollout Will Stall at 3 Months

Month 1: The demo is magic. Leadership is excited. The team is energised.

Month 2: The edge cases arrive. The integration debt surfaces. Someone asks why it's costing 4x the estimate.

Month 3: The original champion moves to another project. The documentation was in one person's head. The prompts that "just worked" now break every other Tuesday. The team quietly reverts to the old process.

I've watched this pattern repeat across companies of every size. The technology wasn't the problem. The maintenance model was.

AI systems aren't like traditional software you ship and own. They're more like gardens — they need constant tending. Model updates change behaviour. Data shifts underneath you. The assumptions baked into your prompts age faster than your codebase.

If you're planning an AI rollout, budget for:
- A dedicated owner, not a side project
- Monthly prompt review cycles
- Re-training or fine-tuning cadence before you need it
- A kill switch that actually gets tested

The teams that succeed treat month 3 as the starting line, not the finish.
