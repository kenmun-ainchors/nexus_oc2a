# LI-W2-P5 — "The 92% rule"

## Slot
Wed 24 Jun 12:00 AEST

## Draft

---

I dumped every rule from every agent into one document.

I expected a manageable list. I found a long one.

Then I deduplicated by intent. Not by text. By intent: what behaviour was this rule actually trying to enforce?

The result: fewer than two dozen unique rules.

That meant almost everything else was redundant. Worse: slight variations of the same rule, written at different times, by different versions of me. Each one drifting slightly from the others.

One agent had "be direct." Another had "be direct, briefly." Another had "be direct and kind."

Three versions of "direct." Three different behaviours. Same intent, different outcomes.

This is how a workflow rots. Not from one bad rule. From many small copies of good rules, none of which are quite the same.

I called it the 92% rule. Because when I counted, roughly 92% of the rule instances in the system were either duplicates or near-duplicates of a small core set.

The fix was not more rules. It was one rule. One definition. One place.

Version them. Deduplicate them. Audit them regularly. Treat rules like production code. Because that's exactly what they are.

**What I learned:** Rule duplication is the silent killer of consistency. Two agents with slightly different versions of the same rule will diverge over time. They will produce different outputs from the same input. And you will spend hours chasing ghosts.

**What I do now:** One source of truth for every rule. Update the source, not the copies. The copies are generated, not maintained.
