## "The quiet cost of running AI without a foundation"

Some time back, a team we work with noticed their AI tooling costs bending upward sharply. The bill was the symptom. The cause was underneath.

They had built workflows around a single model. When that model shifted — pricing, behaviour, quality — the workflows didn't shift with it. The result was a compound hit: higher cost, slower output, more retries, and a growing gap between what the system promised and what it delivered.

What we helped them see: the cost was exposing a foundation problem, not creating one.

Three questions every team should ask:

1. Does your workflow work if the model underneath changes? If the answer is no, the model is your foundation. It shouldn't be.
2. Do you measure cost per outcome, or just cost per call? The first tells you if the system is healthy. The second just tells you if it's running.
3. When the bill changes trajectory, do you patch or do you audit? Patching buys time. Auditing finds the leak.

The teams that get this right treat the model as a replaceable component, not a foundation. The foundation is the spec, the discipline, the governance. The model is the engine. Engines change. Foundations shouldn't have to.

This is the work most teams skip because it's invisible until it breaks. By then, the cost is already loud.
