# LI-W3-P8 — "What 'context discipline' actually means"

## Slot
Wed 1 Jul 12:00 AEST

## Draft

---

Everyone talks about context windows. Nobody talks about context discipline. The second one matters more.

Context window is a number. Context discipline is a behaviour.

Context window says: the model can hold a lot. Context discipline says: I will not give it more than I have to. I will measure. I will prune. I will cache. I will rehydrate only what I need.

When I started measuring, I found something uncomfortable.

A large share of the context in every call was the same boilerplate. System prompts. Rules. History. Re-sent every time, unchanged, taking up the budget the actual work needed.

More was redundant or stale. State that had already served its purpose, still in the call because nobody had built the cleanup.

Only a small fraction of the call was the actual work. The rest was repetition the model had already seen.

After a focused stretch of optimisation, that boilerplate shrank dramatically. The redundancy shrank. The actual work became most of the call. And the latency dropped with it. Less to read, less to think about, faster answers.

Same model. Same output quality. A small fraction of the tokens. That is not a model win. That is a discipline win.

The model did not get smarter. I stopped wasting its attention. The system got faster because I stopped asking the model to re-read its own past on every call.

Most of what fills a context window is repetition the AI has already seen. Trim the repetition. The model gets sharper because it is not drowning in its own history.

Measure your context. Most teams have no idea what fills the call. Once you measure, you cannot unsee it.
