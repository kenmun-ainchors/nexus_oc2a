I've been quiet. Not because nothing was happening. Because everything was.

Recently my AI workflow hit a wall. Not a small one. A foundation-level one.

The bill bent upward. Models I'd designed around drifted. Workarounds piled on workarounds. The system looked like it was working. It wasn't.

So I stopped shipping and started digging.

What I found was a foundation problem. The model I trusted was the model I'd over-trusted. The spec was scattered. The rules were duplicated. The quality gates existed in a document, not in the code. The audit I'd been avoiding was the audit that would matter most.

I tore it down. Rebuilt from the foundation. Memory layer. Task queue. Single source of truth. Governance stack. Context discipline. Spec as production code.

It worked.

Over the next four weeks, I'm going to walk through what broke, what I audited, what I rebuilt, and what shifted in how I work. Four movements:

I. The Cracks. What broke at the visible layer.
II. The Audit. What I found when I looked underneath.
III. The Rebuild. The foundation, layer by layer.
IV. The Shift. What works now, and what I learned.

If you've ever had a system that was "almost working" and couldn't figure out why, this is for you.
