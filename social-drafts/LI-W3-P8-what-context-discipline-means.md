What "context discipline" actually means

Everyone talks about context windows. Nobody talks about context discipline. The second one matters more.

Context window is a number. Context discipline is a behaviour.

Context window says: "the model can hold a lot." Context discipline says: "I will not give it more than I have to. I will measure. I will prune. I will cache. I will rehydrate only what I need."

When I started measuring, I found a large share of context in every call was the same boilerplate. System prompts. Rules. History. More was redundant or stale. Only a small fraction was the actual work.

After a focused stretch of optimisation, that boilerplate shrank dramatically. The redundancy shrank. The actual work became most of the call.

Same model. Same output quality. A small fraction of the tokens. That's not a model win. That's a discipline win.

Most of what fills a context window is repetition the AI has already seen. Trim the repetition. The model gets sharper because it's not drowning in its own history.

Measure your context. Most teams have no idea what fills the call. Once you measure, you can't unsee it.
